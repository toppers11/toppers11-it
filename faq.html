Exam Information
Details about the departmental examination pattern, dates and eligibility.

As per CBDT Notification II dated 14.05.2025, the ITO/ITI Departmental Examination 2025 is scheduled as follows:

  • Paper 1 (IT Law & Computation): Monday, 08.09.2025
  • Paper 2 (Adv. Accountancy / Book Keeping): Tuesday, 09.09.2025
  • Paper 3 (Allied Laws): Wednesday, 10.09.2025
  • Paper 4 (Combined Practical / Office Procedure): Thursday, 11.09.2025

Application window: 23 May 2025 to 23 June 2025 at www.incometaxindia.gov.in.

The Departmental Examination 2025 for ITO and ITI is held in Online Mode (Computer-Based Test), as per the Amended Departmental Examination Rules 2009 (effective from 2023 examination onwards). The only exception is:

  • ITO Paper 4 (Income Tax & Accountancy — Combined Practical) — This is conducted in Offline/Subjective mode, with answers written in a physical booklet provided by the department. Books allowed: IT Act & Rules, CBDT Publications, Ready Reckoner.
  • MS Paper 2 (Office Procedure) — Also conducted with books allowed.

For all objective papers: 1/8th mark (0.125) is deducted for every incorrect answer. This applies to ITI, ITO and MS objective papers.

📊 Score Formula: (Correct × 1) − (Wrong × 0.125) = Final Score
Skipped questions carry zero marks — no deduction.

Toppers11 implements this exact scheme in all our mock tests so you get accurate score predictions.

As per the CBDT Notification 2025, the maximum number of chances for appearing in the departmental examination is limited to 10 for all categories of candidates, including SC/ST candidates availing betterment chance.

Important: Candidates who have already qualified a paper/examination are not eligible to re-appear in that paper, except for betterment candidates who have qualified the entire examination as a reserved category candidate.

  • Paper 1: Income Tax Law & Computation — 150 MCQs, 150 Marks, 3 Hours (Objective, without books)
  • Paper 2: Book Keeping (Accounts) — 100 MCQs, 100 Marks, 2 Hours
  • Paper 3: Allied Laws — 100 MCQs, 100 Marks, 2 Hours
  • Paper 4: Office Procedure — 100 MCQs, 100 Marks, 2 Hours

All papers are objective type (MCQ) without books, except where mentioned. Negative marking 1/8th applies to all.

Syllabus & Topics
Chapter-wise breakdowns for each paper across MS, ITI and ITO exams.

Part A (Approx. 55 Marks):

  • General Terminologies, Tax Rates, Surcharge and Cess
  • Income under head Salary
  • Income under head House Property
  • Income under head PGBP (Profit & Gain from Business/Profession)
  • Income under head Capital Gain
  • Income under head Other Sources (IOS)
  • Deductions under Section 80
  • Advance Tax, Interest u/s 234A/B/C
  • Set-off and Carry Forward of Losses
  • TDS and TCS
  • Taxability of Partnership Firm and Partners
  • Miscellaneous: Clubbing, Cash Limits (S-68/69/269), Trust Provisions, S-14A, MAT/AMT, Agriculture Income, Residential Status, Deemed Income
  • Black Money, Benami and Wealth Act

Part B (Approx. 45 Marks): Assessment Procedures, Penalties, Settlement Commission, Appeals & Revisions, Advance Rulings, Search & Seizure, Refunds, Tax Recovery, Transfer Pricing, Other Topics.

ITI Allied Laws (100 Marks):

  • Transfer of Property Act (~13 marks)

  • The Indian Registration Act, 1908 (~12 marks)
  • The Indian Trust Act, 1882 (~13 marks)
  • The Indian Succession Act, 1925 (~20 marks)
  • Hindu Law (~14 marks)

Rules, General Financial Rules.
